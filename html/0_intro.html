Overview

Mawlana Bhashani Science and Technology University (MBSTU) is a prominent public university, established in 2001 to fill a critical need for specialized education in science and technology disciplines in Bangladesh. Located in Tangail, a region historically significant for its cultural heritage, the university is named after Mawlana Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani, an esteemed political leader and advocate for the underprivileged, reflecting its mission to promote inclusive and sustainable development through education. The university officially commenced academic activities in 2003 with the first batch of students in two departments: Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) and Information and Communication Technology (ICT). Over the years, MBSTU has expanded significantly, now offering a wide array of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across diverse fields such as engineering, natural sciences, life sciences, and business. The university's sprawling 80-acre campus provides scenic beauty of lush greenery and lakes with modern infrastructure, including advanced laboratories, research facilities, accommodation facilities and a central library, fostering an environment of academic excellence and innovation. True to its namesake's legacy, MBSTU emphasizes social responsibility, equitable access to education, and research that addresses national and global challenges. Through its commitment to sustainability, technological advancement, and societal progress, MBSTU has established itself as a beacon of knowledge, preparing students to excel in academia, industry, and leadership roles.
